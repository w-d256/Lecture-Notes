## Chapter 1 — Review of Measure Theory

### Construction of measures

1. **Semiring versus semialgebra.** The original definition called a class a semialgebra without requiring it to contain the whole space and later treated bounded half-open intervals as an algebra. I separated the notions: a semiring has the finite-intersection and finite-disjoint-difference properties; a semialgebra is a semiring that also contains `\Omega`. Bounded half-open intervals on `\mathbb R` are therefore correctly classified as a semiring.
2. **Generated ring/algebra.** The original generated-family formula did not distinguish the ring generated by a semiring from the algebra generated by a semialgebra. I proved that finite disjoint unions form the generated ring and explained when it is also an algebra.
3. **Premeasure definition.** The original “pre-measurable space” definition mixed a space, an algebra, and a set function and did not state the requirement that the countable union remain in the domain. I replaced it with the standard definition on a ring or semiring.
4. **Extension from a semiring.** The original extension formula did not prove independence of the chosen disjoint representation. I added a common-refinement argument and then proved finite and countable additivity on the generated ring.
5. **No subtraction of infinities.** Several original additivity arguments implicitly subtracted quantities that could be infinite. I rewrote them using disjoint refinements, monotonicity, and nonnegative series only.
6. **Premeasure criterion.** The original “super-additivity” discussion did not provide the criterion actually needed later. I stated and proved the equivalence between finite additivity plus countable subadditivity and the premeasure property.
7. **Lebesgue–Stieltjes interval class.** The interval family, endpoint conventions, and increment formula were made consistent: `\chi_F((a,b])=F(b)-F(a)` on the half-open interval semiring.
8. **Lebesgue–Stieltjes necessity and sufficiency.** The original proof used undefined one-sided values and an incomplete cover argument. I supplied the compact finite-subcover proof of subadditivity under right continuity and a non-circular disjoint-series proof that a premeasure forces right continuity.
9. **Outer-measure construction.** I corrected the covering family, the range, the convention `\inf\varnothing=\infty`, and the use of countable covers, then supplied the epsilon-cover proof of countable subadditivity.
10. **Agreement with the premeasure.** The original reverse inequality used only finite additivity against a countable cover. I disjointified the cover, intersected it with the target set, and used countable additivity correctly.
11. **Carathéodory measurability.** The criterion was rewritten with consistent set difference and quantified over every subset of the ambient space.
12. **Carathéodory sigma-algebra proof.** The original proof did not justify arbitrary countable unions or countable additivity. I added the finite-iteration inequality, passage to a disjoint countable union, and the restriction argument.
13. **Completeness.** The original null-subset argument used the wrong set operation. I proved directly that every subset of a measurable null set satisfies the Carathéodory equality for every test set.
14. **Extension theorem.** The existence statement is now formulated for a premeasure on a ring and restricted to `\sigma(\mathcal R)`.
15. **Uniqueness hypothesis.** The original uniqueness proof used complements as if every extension were finite. I added the required sigma-finite exhaustion `E_k\uparrow\Omega`, restricted both measures to `E_k`, and used a Dynkin-system argument followed by continuity from below.

### Radon measures and Lebesgue measure

16. **Continuity of measures.** I collected continuity from below, continuity from above with a finite first set, and the converse premeasure criteria. The original statement omitted or mixed the required finiteness assumptions.
17. **Radon definition.** The original notes alternated between local finiteness and regularity as though either alone were the definition. I stated the locally compact Hausdorff definition and the equivalent metric-space regularity formulation.
18. **Euclidean regularity.** The original proof was incomplete and duplicated later definitions. I gave a finite-measure regularity class argument and explained the locally finite reduction by compact exhaustion.
19. **Distribution function sign.** For `x<0`, the original formula had the wrong sign, so its alleged distribution function was not nondecreasing. I changed it to `-\mu((x,0])` and verified the increment identity across zero.
20. **Right continuity of the distribution function.** I used continuity from above on finite local intervals rather than an unproved heuristic.
21. **Converse Stieltjes construction.** I stated uniqueness as uniqueness of the locally finite measure determined by the increments, not uniqueness of the function up to an additive constant.
22. **Uniqueness of Lebesgue measure.** The original result had spelling errors and an unfinished proof. I derived the measure of rational intervals, extended to real endpoints by continuity, and invoked uniqueness on the generating half-open intervals.

### Random variables and measurability

23. **Pullback versus pushforward.** The original called a law a pullback. I separated inverse-image families from the pushforward law `f_\#P`.
24. **Generated inverse images.** I proved `f^{-1}(\sigma(\mathcal C))=\sigma(f^{-1}(\mathcal C))`, replacing several overlapping and partially stated measurability criteria.
25. **Doob–Dynkin factorization.** The original proof stopped after choosing sublevel sets and did not define a valid real-valued factor on all of the target space. I constructed the rational-sublevel infimum, verified measurability, and assigned a harmless value off the finite-valued set.
26. **Standard-Borel extension.** I added the correct statement for targets that are standard Borel, using a Borel isomorphism rather than claiming the factorization for arbitrary codomains without qualification.
27. **Integration against a law.** I made the pushforward integral identity precise for nonnegative and integrable functions and included the exact integrability equivalence.
28. **First Borel–Cantelli event.** The original identified “infinitely often” with a liminf. I corrected it to the limsup `\bigcap_m\bigcup_{n\ge m}A_n` and supplied the tail-union proof.
29. **Differentiation under the integral sign.** The original omitted the parameter domain, section measurability/integrability, and an integrable derivative bound. I added all three hypotheses and justified the derivative by dominated convergence applied to difference quotients.
30. **Functional monotone-class setup.** The original definitions of bounded-convergence closure, measurable bounded functions, and multiplicative classes were inconsistent. I consolidated them into one precise framework.
31. **Product closure in the functional theorem.** The original proof assumed the desired product closure. I proved it by fixing one factor and applying the minimality of the bounded-convergence class twice.
32. **Indicator generation.** I added polynomial approximation, continuous cutoff approximations to rational sublevel indicators, the Dynkin-system argument, and the final simple-function approximation.
33. **Borel sigma-field generated by `C_c`.** The original distance-function argument failed for `U=\mathbb R^d` and did not ensure compact support. I treated the whole-space case separately and multiplied the distance function by compactly supported cutoffs.
34. **Determination by test functions.** I made the proof explicit by adjoining the constant function and stated an equal-total-mass condition; for probability measures it is automatic. For finite Radon measures that mass equality can also be recovered from compact cutoffs, so the corrected statement is slightly conservative but fully valid. The proof now uses the functional monotone-class theorem correctly.

### Product measures and Kolmogorov extension

35. **Product sigma-algebra.** I replaced malformed braces and finite-dimensional-only notation by the coordinate-cylinder definition for an arbitrary index set.
36. **Coordinate measurability.** I gave the exact if-and-only-if criterion and its one-line generator proof.
37. **Tonelli–Fubini theorem.** The original mixed existence, section measurability, nonnegative integration, and integrable integration. I separated the cases and stated absolute integrability before interchanging signed integrals.
38. **Compact-cube extension theorem.** The original proof asserted compactness without producing a positive finite-intersection estimate. I chose compact subcylinders using finite-dimensional regularity, controlled the discarded mass, proved the finite intersection property, and thereby established continuity at the empty set.
39. **Cylinder uniqueness.** I made explicit that the cylinder sets form a generating pi-system, so the finite-dimensional marginals determine the measure.
40. **Standard Borel definition.** The original equated “Polish” and “standard Borel.” I defined a standard Borel space as a measurable space isomorphic to a Borel subset of a Polish space, equivalently a Borel subset of `[0,1]`.
41. **Countable standard-Borel products.** I transported the finite-dimensional laws to Borel subsets of `[0,1]`, applied the compact-cube result, proved the product support has full measure, and transported the measure back.
42. **Infinite product measures.** I corrected the cylinder formula and wrote the finite-coordinate integral identity as a pushforward statement.
43. **Independent versus identically distributed.** The original conclusion called the coordinate variables i.i.d. even when their prescribed laws differed. I changed it to “independent,” with identical distribution precisely when all marginal measures coincide.
